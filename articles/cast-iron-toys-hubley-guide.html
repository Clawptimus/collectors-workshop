Cast Iron Toys of Hubley

Identification and value guide for American-made cast iron playthings

The Hubley Manufacturing Company

Founded in 1894 in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, the Hubley Manufacturing Company produced some of the finest cast iron toys in America. For nearly 80 years, Hubley created durable, detailed playthings that have become highly prized by collectors today. A more complete guide can be found in Cast Iron Toys of Hubley is available here.

Hubley's toys stand out for their quality of casting, their attention to detail, and their variety. From motorcycles and airplanes to cap pistols and doorstops, Hubley produced an astonishing range of cast iron objects that captured the American imagination.

Major Hubley Categories

Vehicles: Hubley's cars, trucks, motorcycles, and airplanes are among their most collectible pieces. The "Indian" motorcycle with rider is an icon of American toy collecting. Look for variations in color, wheels, and rider position.

Cap pistols: Hubley produced an enormous range of cast iron cap guns, from simple single-shot models to elaborate revolvers with working actions. The "Texan" and "Rodeo" series are particularly sought after.

Animals: Hubley's animal figures, from dogs and horses to circus animals, display remarkable detail. Many were designed as doorstops or bookends rather than toys, and these functional pieces are highly collectible.

Airplanes: Hubley produced aviation toys from the Wright Brothers era through the jet age. Their Lindbergh-era planes and WWII military aircraft are particularly prized.

Identifying Hubley Toys

Markings: Many Hubley pieces are marked with the company name, though the location and style of the marking varies. Look on the underside of vehicles, on the grips of pistols, and on the base of figures.

Quality of casting: Hubley toys have crisp detail and smooth surfaces. Poor casting quality, rough edges, or loss of detail may indicate a reproduction or a lower-quality competitor.

Paint: Original Hubley paint has a distinctive character. The colors were vibrant when new, and surviving examples often show honest play wear. Be wary of pieces that look too perfect — they may have been repainted.

Hardware: Wheels, axles, and other hardware can help identify Hubley pieces. The company used specific styles of wheels and attachments that changed over time.

Value Factors

Rarity: Some Hubley toys were produced in limited quantities or for short periods. The rarer the piece, the more valuable it tends to be. Reference books and price guides can help identify scarce models.

Condition: As with all toys, condition matters. Original paint in excellent condition commands a premium. However, cast iron toys were meant to be played with, and honest wear is acceptable — even desirable — to many collectors.

Completeness: Missing wheels, broken parts, or replaced hardware all affect value. Complete, original examples are always worth more than incomplete or restored pieces.

Documentation: Original boxes, catalogs, or other documentation add significant value and interest to Hubley pieces.
